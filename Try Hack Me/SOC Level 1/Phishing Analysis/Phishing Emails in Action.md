# Phishing Emails in Action

## Objective
Analyze real-world phishing email scenarios and learn how SOC analysts identify malicious emails, investigate suspicious indicators, and recognize common phishing techniques used by attackers.

---

## Concepts Covered
- Phishing email analysis
- Social engineering
- Email investigation
- URL analysis
- Attachment analysis
- Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)

---

## Phishing Email Overview

Phishing emails are designed to trick users into:
- Revealing credentials
- Downloading malware
- Clicking malicious links
- Sharing sensitive information

Attackers often impersonate trusted organizations to increase the likelihood of success.

---

## Email Investigation Process

### Sender Analysis
Reviewed sender information to identify:
- Spoofed email addresses
- Suspicious domains
- Unusual reply-to addresses

---

### Subject & Content Analysis
Analyzed email content for:
- Urgent language
- Threatening messages
- Requests for sensitive information
- Social engineering tactics

---

### URL Analysis
Investigated embedded links to identify:
- Malicious domains
- Fake login pages
- Suspicious redirects
- Typosquatting attempts

Important checks included:
- Domain legitimacy
- URL structure
- HTTPS usage

---

### Attachment Analysis
Reviewed suspicious attachments that could contain:
- Malware
- Malicious macros
- Credential stealers
- Executable payloads

---

## Indicators of Phishing

Common phishing indicators identified:
- Unexpected emails
- Urgent requests
- Suspicious links
- Spelling or grammar mistakes
- Fake branding
- Unusual attachments

---

## SOC Investigation Workflow

Typical phishing investigation process:
- Review suspicious email
- Analyze sender details
- Inspect URLs and attachments
- Identify Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)
- Escalate confirmed phishing attempts

---

## Best Practices

Recommended defensive measures:
- Verify sender authenticity
- Avoid opening suspicious attachments
- Hover over links before clicking
- Use multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Report phishing emails promptly
- Conduct user awareness training

---

## Key Learnings
- Phishing attacks rely heavily on social engineering
- Email analysis requires careful inspection of headers, links, and attachments
- Suspicious URLs are a common phishing indicator
- SOC analysts must validate phishing alerts accurately
- User awareness plays a critical role in phishing prevention
