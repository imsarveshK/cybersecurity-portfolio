# Humans as Attack Vectors

## Objective
Understand how humans can become attack vectors in cybersecurity incidents and learn the common social engineering techniques used by attackers to compromise organizations.

---

## Concepts Covered
- Social Engineering
- Human attack vectors
- Phishing attacks
- Insider threats
- Psychological manipulation
- Security awareness

---

## Human Attack Vectors

Attackers often target people instead of systems because human behavior can be easier to exploit than technical defenses.

Common reasons include:
- Lack of security awareness
- Trust exploitation
- Curiosity or urgency
- Weak password practices

---

## Social Engineering Techniques

### Phishing
Fraudulent emails or messages designed to trick users into:
- Revealing credentials
- Opening malicious attachments
- Visiting malicious websites

### Spear Phishing
Targeted phishing attacks aimed at specific individuals or organizations using personalized information.

---

### Vishing
Voice-based social engineering attacks conducted through phone calls.

---

### Smishing
SMS-based phishing attacks designed to trick users into clicking malicious links or sharing information.

---

## Insider Threats

Insider threats may involve:
- Malicious employees
- Negligent users
- Compromised accounts

Potential risks include:
- Data theft
- Unauthorized access
- Information leakage

---

## Indicators of Social Engineering Attacks

Common warning signs:
- Urgent requests
- Suspicious links
- Unexpected attachments
- Requests for sensitive information
- Unusual sender addresses

---

## Security Awareness Best Practices

Recommended defensive measures:
- Security awareness training
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Strong password policies
- Verifying suspicious requests
- Reporting phishing attempts
