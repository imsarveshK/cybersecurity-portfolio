# Greenholt Phish Challenge

## Objective
Investigate a phishing incident within a simulated SOC environment by analyzing suspicious emails, identifying phishing indicators, and applying phishing analysis techniques to determine malicious activity.

---

## Concepts Covered
- Phishing investigation
- Email analysis
- Social engineering
- URL analysis
- Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)
- Security monitoring

---

## Challenge Overview

The Greenholt Phish Challenge simulated a phishing investigation scenario where suspicious emails required analysis to determine whether malicious activity was present.

The challenge focused on:
- Investigating phishing emails
- Identifying suspicious indicators
- Analyzing email content
- Reviewing malicious URLs and attachments
- Applying SOC investigation workflows

---

## Investigation Workflow

### Email Analysis
Reviewed suspicious emails to identify:
- Spoofed sender addresses
- Social engineering tactics
- Suspicious language
- Malicious indicators

---

### URL & Attachment Investigation
Analyzed:
- Embedded URLs
- Suspicious domains
- Potential malicious attachments
- Phishing-related infrastructure

---

### IOC Identification
Investigated Indicators of Compromise (IOCs) such as:
- Malicious domains
- Suspicious IP addresses
- Email artifacts
- Suspicious URLs

---

## SOC Skills Applied

The challenge involved:
- Phishing email investigation
- Threat analysis
- Alert triage
- IOC validation
- Incident escalation concepts

---

## Key Learnings
- Phishing attacks rely heavily on social engineering techniques
- Email analysis requires careful inspection of sender details and links
- IOC validation improves phishing investigations
- SOC analysts must investigate suspicious emails efficiently
- Threat intelligence supports phishing detection and analysis
