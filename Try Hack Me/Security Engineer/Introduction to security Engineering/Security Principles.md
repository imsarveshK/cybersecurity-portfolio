# Security Principles

## Objective
Understand the fundamental principles of cybersecurity, including core security concepts, defensive strategies, and best practices used to protect systems, networks, and organizational assets.

---

## Concepts Covered
- Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability (CIA Triad)
- Authentication
- Authorization
- Non-repudiation
- Defense in depth
- Least privilege
- Risk management

---

## Security Principles Overview

Security principles provide the foundation for designing and maintaining secure systems and environments.

These principles help organizations:
- Protect sensitive information
- Reduce security risks
- Maintain operational stability
- Improve defensive security posture

---

## CIA Triad

### Confidentiality
Ensures sensitive information is accessible only to authorized users.

Examples:
- Encryption
- Access controls
- Data classification

---

### Integrity
Ensures data remains accurate and unmodified.

Examples:
- Hashing
- File integrity monitoring
- Digital signatures

---

### Availability
Ensures systems and data remain accessible when needed.

Examples:
- Redundancy
- Backups
- Disaster recovery planning

---

## Authentication

Authentication verifies the identity of users or systems attempting to access resources.

Examples:
- Passwords
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Biometrics

---

## Authorization

Authorization determines what authenticated users are allowed to access or perform.

Examples:
- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
- Permissions management
- Access policies

---

## Non-Repudiation

Ensures that actions or communications cannot be denied by the responsible party.

Examples:
- Digital signatures
- Logging and auditing

---

## Defense in Depth

A layered security approach that uses multiple defensive controls to protect systems and infrastructure.

Examples:
- Firewalls
- Endpoint protection
- IDS/IPS solutions
- Security monitoring

---

## Principle of Least Privilege

Users and systems should only have the minimum permissions necessary to perform required tasks.

Benefits include:
- Reduced attack surface
- Limited attacker movement
- Lower risk of misuse

---

## Risk Management

Organizations identify and manage security risks by:
- Assessing vulnerabilities
- Evaluating threats
- Applying mitigations
- Monitoring security controls

---

## Best Practices

Recommended approaches:
- Implement least privilege access
- Use layered security controls
- Enable MFA where possible
- Keep systems updated
- Monitor systems continuously
- Conduct regular risk assessments

---

## Key Learnings
- Security principles form the foundation of cybersecurity
- The CIA Triad is central to protecting information systems
- Authentication and authorization improve access security
- Defense in depth strengthens organizational resilience
- Least privilege reduces the impact of compromise
