# SOC Roles in the Blue Team

## Objective
Understand the different roles within a Blue Team environment and learn how Security Operations Center (SOC) teams work together to detect, analyze, and respond to cybersecurity threats.

---

## Concepts Covered
- Blue Team operations
- Security Operations Center (SOC)
- SOC Analyst responsibilities
- Threat Intelligence
- Digital Forensics and Incident Response (DFIR)
- Security monitoring and escalation

---

## Blue Team Overview

The Blue Team is responsible for defending an organization's infrastructure against cyber threats.  
Their responsibilities include:
- Monitoring systems and networks
- Detecting malicious activity
- Responding to incidents
- Improving overall security posture

---

## Security Operations Center (SOC)

A SOC is a centralized team responsible for:
- Continuous security monitoring
- Alert investigation
- Incident response coordination
- Threat detection and analysis

SOC teams typically operate 24/7 to ensure rapid identification of threats.

### Tier 1 Analyst
- Monitor security alerts
- Perform initial investigation
- Triage suspicious activity
- Escalate confirmed threats

### Tier 2 Analyst
- Conduct deeper analysis
- Investigate complex incidents
- Correlate events from multiple sources

### Tier 3 Analyst
- Perform advanced threat hunting
- Handle sophisticated attacks
- Develop detection rules and improvements

---

## Threat Intelligence

Threat Intelligence helps SOC teams:
- Identify Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)
- Track attacker techniques
- Improve detection capabilities

Examples of IOCs:
- Malicious IP addresses
- Suspicious domains
- File hashes
- Malicious URLs

---

## DFIR (Digital Forensics and Incident Response)

DFIR teams focus on:
- Investigating security incidents
- Collecting forensic evidence
- Determining attack scope
- Supporting recovery efforts
