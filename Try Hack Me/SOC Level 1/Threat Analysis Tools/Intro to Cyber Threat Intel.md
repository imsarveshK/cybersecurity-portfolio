# Intro to Cyber Threat Intelligence

## Objective
Understand the fundamentals of Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI), including how threat intelligence is collected, analyzed, and used by security teams to identify and respond to cyber threats.

---

## Concepts Covered
- Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI)
- Threat intelligence lifecycle
- Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)
- Threat actors
- Threat analysis
- Intelligence sources

---

## Cyber Threat Intelligence Overview

Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) involves collecting and analyzing information about:
- Threat actors
- Attack techniques
- Malicious infrastructure
- Emerging cyber threats

CTI helps organizations improve detection capabilities and strengthen defensive security operations.

---

## Importance of Threat Intelligence

Threat intelligence helps security teams:
- Identify malicious activity
- Understand attacker behavior
- Improve threat detection
- Prioritize security risks
- Support incident response investigations

---

## Threat Intelligence Lifecycle

The threat intelligence process typically includes:

### Direction
Define intelligence goals and requirements.

---

### Collection
Gather threat-related information from various sources.

Examples:
- Security feeds
- Open-source intelligence (OSINT)
- Threat reports
- Malware analysis

---

### Processing
Organize and structure collected data for analysis.

---

### Analysis
Analyze collected information to identify:
- Threat patterns
- Attacker behavior
- Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)

---

### Dissemination
Share intelligence findings with security teams and stakeholders.

---

### Feedback
Review intelligence effectiveness and improve processes.

---

## Types of Threat Intelligence

### Strategic Intelligence
High-level intelligence focused on organizational risks and trends.

---

### Tactical Intelligence
Information about attacker tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs).

---

### Operational Intelligence
Details about ongoing attacks and campaigns.

---

### Technical Intelligence
Specific technical indicators such as:
- Malicious IP addresses
- Domains
- File hashes

---

## Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)

Common IOCs include:
- Suspicious IP addresses
- Malicious domains
- File hashes
- Suspicious URLs
- Malware signatures

---

## Threat Intelligence Sources

Examples of intelligence sources:
- OSINT platforms
- Threat feeds
- Malware repositories
- Security vendor reports
- MITRE ATT&CK framework

---

## Best Practices

Recommended approaches:
- Validate intelligence before action
- Correlate intelligence with security events
- Continuously update threat feeds
- Share intelligence across teams
- Use CTI to improve detection strategies

---

## Key Learnings
- CTI improves organizational threat awareness
- Threat intelligence supports incident investigations
- IOCs help identify malicious activity
- Understanding attacker behavior improves defenses
- Threat intelligence is a continuous process
