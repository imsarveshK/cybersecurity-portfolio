# Cyber Kill Chain

## Objective
Understand the stages of the Cyber Kill Chain framework and learn how attackers conduct cyber attacks through a structured sequence of actions.

## Cyber Kill Chain Overview

The Cyber Kill Chain is a cybersecurity framework developed by Lockheed Martin to describe the stages of a cyber attack from initial reconnaissance to achieving attacker objectives.

The framework helps defenders:
- Understand attacker behavior
- Detect attacks earlier
- Improve incident response
- Disrupt adversary operations

---

## Stages of the Cyber Kill Chain

### 1. Reconnaissance
Attackers gather information about the target.

Examples:
- OSINT collection
- Scanning public systems
- Identifying employees
- Domain enumeration

---

### 2. Weaponization
Attackers prepare malicious payloads or exploit tools.

Examples:
- Malware creation
- Malicious document preparation
- Exploit development

---

### 3. Delivery
The malicious payload is delivered to the target.

Examples:
- Phishing emails
- Malicious attachments
- Drive-by downloads
- USB devices

---

### 4. Exploitation
Attackers exploit vulnerabilities to execute malicious code.

Examples:
- Software vulnerabilities
- Credential abuse
- Remote code execution

---

### 5. Installation
Malware or persistence mechanisms are installed on the compromised system.

Examples:
- Backdoors
- Persistence scripts
- Scheduled tasks

---

### 6. Command and Control (C2)
Compromised systems communicate with attacker-controlled infrastructure.

Examples:
- Remote access channels
- Beaconing activity
- Encrypted communications

---

### 7. Actions on Objectives
Attackers achieve their final goals.

Examples:
- Data exfiltration
- Ransomware deployment
- Credential theft
- Lateral movement

---

## Importance for SOC Analysts

SOC analysts use the Cyber Kill Chain to:
- Identify attack stages
- Detect suspicious activity
- Improve monitoring strategies
- Disrupt attacks before completion

---

## Defensive Strategies

Recommended approaches:
- Monitor reconnaissance activity
- Detect phishing attempts
- Patch vulnerable systems
- Block malicious communications
- Monitor lateral movement behavior
- Implement endpoint protection
