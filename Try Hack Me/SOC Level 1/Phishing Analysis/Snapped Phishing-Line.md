# Snapped Phishing Line Challenge

## Objective
Investigate a phishing-related security incident by analyzing suspicious activity, identifying phishing indicators, and applying SOC investigation techniques within a simulated environment.

---

## Concepts Covered
- Phishing investigation
- Alert triage
- Email analysis
- Incident response
- Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)
- Threat analysis

---

## Challenge Overview

The Snapped Phishing Line Challenge simulated a phishing incident requiring investigation and analysis of suspicious activity within a SOC environment.

The challenge focused on:
- Investigating phishing-related alerts
- Identifying malicious indicators
- Analyzing suspicious communications
- Reviewing attack evidence
- Applying incident response concepts

---

## Investigation Workflow

### Initial Alert Review
Performed initial triage by:
- Reviewing suspicious activity
- Gathering contextual information
- Identifying affected users or systems
- Assessing alert severity

---

### Phishing Analysis
Investigated phishing indicators including:
- Suspicious sender information
- Malicious URLs
- Social engineering techniques
- Potential credential theft attempts

---

### IOC Investigation
Analyzed Indicators of Compromise (IOCs) such as:
- Malicious domains
- Suspicious IP addresses
- Email artifacts
- Malicious links

---

## SOC Skills Applied

The challenge involved:
- Phishing email investigation
- Security event analysis
- IOC validation
- Alert prioritization
- Incident escalation workflows

---

## Key Learnings
- Phishing attacks often rely on social engineering and deceptive communication
- Analysts must validate suspicious indicators carefully
- IOC correlation improves phishing investigations
- Effective triage helps prioritize incidents efficiently
- Threat intelligence supports phishing detection and response
