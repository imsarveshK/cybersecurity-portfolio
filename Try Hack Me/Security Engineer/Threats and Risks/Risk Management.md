# Risk Management

## Objective
Understand the fundamentals of risk management, including identifying, assessing, and mitigating cybersecurity risks to reduce the impact of threats on organizational assets and operations.

---

## Concepts Covered
- Risk management
- Threats and vulnerabilities
- Risk assessment
- Risk mitigation
- Security controls
- Business impact analysis

---

## Risk Management Overview

Risk management is the process of identifying, evaluating, and reducing risks that could negatively impact an organization’s systems, data, or operations.

The goal is to minimize the likelihood and impact of security incidents while maintaining business operations effectively.

---

## Key Risk Components

### Assets
Anything valuable to an organization that requires protection.

Examples:
- Systems
- Databases
- User accounts
- Intellectual property
- Sensitive information

---

### Threats
Potential events or actors capable of causing harm.

Examples:
- Malware
- Phishing attacks
- Insider threats
- Natural disasters

---

### Vulnerabilities
Weaknesses that attackers can exploit.

Examples:
- Unpatched systems
- Weak passwords
- Misconfigurations
- Lack of access controls

---

## Risk Assessment Process

Organizations assess risks by:
1. Identifying assets
2. Identifying threats and vulnerabilities
3. Evaluating likelihood and impact
4. Determining risk level
5. Applying mitigation strategies

---

## Risk Treatment Strategies

### Risk Mitigation
Reducing risk through security controls.

Examples:
- Firewalls
- MFA
- Patching systems

---

### Risk Transfer
Shifting risk to another party.

Examples:
- Cyber insurance
- Third-party services

---

### Risk Acceptance
Accepting risk when the impact or likelihood is low or mitigation costs are too high.

---

### Risk Avoidance
Eliminating activities that introduce risk.

Example:
- Disabling vulnerable services

---

## Security Controls

Organizations implement controls to reduce risk.

Examples:
- Access controls
- Security monitoring
- Encryption
- Backup solutions
- Employee training

---

## Importance of Risk Management

Effective risk management helps organizations:
- Reduce security incidents
- Protect critical assets
- Improve business continuity
- Support compliance requirements
- Prioritize security investments

## Best Practices

Recommended approaches:
- Conduct regular risk assessments
- Prioritize critical assets
- Implement layered security controls
- Review risks continuously
- Maintain updated incident response plans

- Risk management is essential for organizational security
- Threats and vulnerabilities contribute to overall risk
- Risk assessments help prioritize security efforts
- Security controls reduce likelihood and impact of incidents
- Continuous monitoring improves risk management effectiveness
