# Phishing Analysis Fundamentals

## Objective
Understand the fundamentals of phishing analysis, including identifying phishing indicators, analyzing suspicious emails, and recognizing common social engineering techniques used in phishing attacks.

---

## Concepts Covered
- Phishing attacks
- Email analysis
- Social engineering
- Suspicious indicators
- Email headers
- URL analysis
- Attachment analysis

---

## Phishing Overview

Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers attempt to trick users into:
- Revealing credentials
- Opening malicious attachments
- Clicking malicious links
- Providing sensitive information

Phishing attacks often impersonate trusted organizations or individuals.

---

## Common Phishing Indicators

Suspicious signs may include:
- Urgent language
- Unusual sender addresses
- Suspicious links
- Grammar or spelling mistakes
- Unexpected attachments
- Requests for sensitive information

---

## Email Header Analysis

Email headers help analysts identify:
- Sender information
- Reply-to addresses
- Mail server paths
- Spoofed domains
- Suspicious routing behavior

Header analysis is important for determining email legitimacy.

---

## URL Analysis

Analysts examine URLs to identify:
- Malicious domains
- Typosquatting
- Suspicious redirects
- Fake login pages

Important checks include:
- Domain reputation
- HTTPS usage
- URL structure

---

## Attachment Analysis

Phishing emails may contain malicious attachments such as:
- Office documents
- PDFs
- Executables
- Compressed files

Attachments may contain:
- Malware
- Macros
- Credential stealers

---

## SOC Investigation Workflow

Typical phishing investigation process:
- Review suspicious email
- Analyze headers
- Inspect URLs and attachments
- Identify Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)
- Escalate confirmed phishing incidents

## Best Practices

Recommended defensive measures:
- Verify sender legitimacy
- Avoid clicking suspicious links
- Scan attachments before opening
- Implement email filtering
- Use multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Conduct security awareness training

---

## Key Learnings
- Phishing relies heavily on social engineering
- Email headers provide valuable investigation data
- Suspicious URLs and attachments are common attack vectors
- Analysts must validate emails carefully before escalation
- User awareness is critical for reducing phishing risk
